From brain data to better care

Data wrestler and physicist with 5+ years’ experience analysing clinical and observational cohort data. I build reproducible pipelines and apply statistical modelling and machine learning to reveal patterns in brain health, from structural/diffusion/functional Magnetic Resonance analytics to eye-tracking phenotyping and translational applications in ageing and neurodegeneration.

In short, I use data to understand brains — and sometimes humans.

My research is based at the University of Newcastle, Australia, and the Hunter Medical Research Institute.

Featured Projects

Modelling

Building scalable computational models to study hippocampal-cortical dynamics and large-scale brain activity.

Eye-tracking + fMRI

Combining eye tracking with MRI during naturalistic viewing to link gaze behaviour with neural activity and attention.

Heat and Health

Investigating how environmental heat impacts cognition and health to identify vulnerability and inform practical interventions for safer ageing.

DWI-Thermometry

Developing multi-shell diffusion MRI thermometry as a reliable, non-invasive biomarker of neuroinflammation and brain metabolism.
