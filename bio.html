About me

I work at the intersection of Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI), computational modelling, machine learning, and clinical translation. My research aims to get more meaningful information about the from using different imaging modalities: from diffusion and functional MRI models that reveal changes in the brain’s architecture, to eye-tracking paradigms that capture cognitive and sensory signatures. I focus on building computational frameworks for large-scale brain activity and digital biomarkers to better detect and monitor brain health across ageing and neurodegeneration.

Currently, I’m affiliated with the University of Newcastle and the Hunter Medical Research Institute (HMRI).

Research Interests

  • MRI analytics (diffusion, fMRI, physiological coupling (e.g. CSF))
  • Eye-movement and behaviour modelling
  • Neural Mass Models for large-scale models of brain activity
  • Translational neuroimaging for ageing and dementia
  • Brain temperature and thermometry
